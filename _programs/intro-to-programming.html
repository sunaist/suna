{{ page.program-name }}

The World Wide Web is together a technology and a universal and influential resource in our society and day to day living nowadays. For one to build well-designed and current websites, students need practical and design skills as well as systematic skills for indulging who is using the web, in what ways they are using it, and for what purpose they will be using it. In this course, students develop skills in all three of these areas through the use of technologies such as HTML, Cascading Style sheets (CSS) and JavaScript. Students study how websites are deployed and used, usability issues on the web, designing website to meet the needs of users, and methods for visual layout and information design.

The course focus more on hands-on and students will be spending the entire time of the course not only learning concepts but applying them in reality as well. The course requires the design and implementation of a simple website at the end of the programming. Prerequisite to this course will be a certificate in Information Technology and Communication.
