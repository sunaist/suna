Peace, and welcome to SUNA.

Mr. Alhagie Jallow is the founder and Chief Executive Officer of Suna Institute of Science and Technology and also an Administrative Information Technology (IT) Staff of the University of The Gambia. He provides leadership to the consultancy services, strategic development, program design and implementation as well as monitoring and evaluation of projects within the institute.

There is a relatively small segment of leaders in the IT and business world; individuals who seem able to build relationships, trust, and rapport with almost anyone, and then are able to enhance corporate relationships and make connections between people, creating partnerships and alliances, and spontaneous motivation in the ‘get things done’ principles.

Mr. Jallow is one of those people, and ‘getting things done’ and driving results - through collaboration, partnerships, and relationships with the institute’s genuine partners and collaborators. With an enthusiastic and genuinely friendly attitude, Alhagie Jallow radiates a sincere passion for delivering value and benefits to his customers and collaborators.

An Information System (IS) Expert by training, Alhagie earned his BSc in Information System at the University of The Gambia in 2013, and spent more than four years as the figurehead of the institution as well as Admin IT staff of the University of The Gambia. He is currently pursuing his MSc in Information Programming at Gaston Berger University, Senegal. Alhagie’s contributions centered on spearheading designing, development and delivery of quality consultancy services and operational programs and improvement activities. But more importantly, the CEO focused on building long-lasting, loyal relationships within the students and customers on developing programs to meet market demands and help deepen market penetration with a view to deliver programs, services, and tailored customer solutions.

Prior to his current professional portfolio, Alhagie worked as the Public Relations Officer of Global Hands - The Gambia for about a term of two years. Promoted twice in two years at the said Organization, in his final role he managed start-up for youth entrepreneurship development programs.

Throughout it all, Mr. Jallow has repeatedly proved his ability to lead through diverse and challenging situations. As an Active Youth Activist, he is an excellent agent of change and has a documented track record of accomplishments that include the designing of student information management systems, ID card projects; creation and launch of new and improved educational and operational programs; and growth and expansion of mature, established operations.
